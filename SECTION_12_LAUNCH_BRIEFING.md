## EXPECTED FINDINGS

### Typical Usability Issues (Examples)

**Navigation & Discoverability**
- Users couldn't find feature X
- Menu was confusing
- Search wasn't obvious

**Forms & Input**
- Error messages unclear
- Password validation too strict
- Form labels missing

**Mobile & Responsive**
- Buttons too small on mobile
- Text unreadable on phone
- Horizontal scrolling present

**Accessibility**
- Can't use keyboard to navigate
- Focus indicator invisible
- Screen reader incompatible

**Performance**
- Page slow to load
- Bid updates felt delayed
- Mobile stuttering

### Report Deliverable
- Executive summary (1 page)
- Findings by category (5 pages)
- Top 10 issues with severity (2 pages)
- Recommended fixes (3 pages)
- Raw data appendix (3+ pages)

## FACILITATOR ROLE: DO'S & DON'Ts

### DO ‚úÖ
- [ ] Be welcoming and warm
- [ ] Ask open-ended questions
- [ ] Listen more than you talk
- [ ] Let them struggle (it's valuable feedback)
- [ ] Take notes on behavior + words
- [ ] Stay neutral about design
- [ ] Thank them sincerely

### DON'T ‚ùå
- [ ] Give hints or help too much
- [ ] Defend design choices
- [ ] Interrupt their process
- [ ] Make them feel bad for struggling
- [ ] Use technical jargon
- [ ] Ask leading questions
- [ ] Distract them with phone

## INTRODUCTION TO READ TO PARTICIPANTS

---

**"Thanks for coming! Here's what happens today:**

**We've built a website called Silent Auction Gallery for school fundraising. Today, I want to watch you use it so we can make it better.**

**Here's the important part: You're not being tested. THE WEBSITE is being tested. There are no right or wrong answers.**

**If something's confusing, that's exactly what we want to know.**

**What will happen:**
1. I'll ask you to complete a few tasks (like browsing auctions or bidding)
2. Think out loud - tell me what you're thinking
3. If you get stuck, that's okay - just tell me
4. You can say "I don't know" or "This is confusing"
5. Takes about an hour

**Questions? Let's begin.**"
