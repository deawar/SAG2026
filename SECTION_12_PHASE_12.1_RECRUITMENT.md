## FACILITATOR GUIDELINES

### DO's âœ…

- [ ] Be welcoming and make participant comfortable
- [ ] Ask open-ended questions: "What are you thinking?"
- [ ] Listen more than you talk
- [ ] Take notes on observations (body language, confusion, delight)
- [ ] Remain neutral (don't defend design)
- [ ] Allow silence - let them think
- [ ] Ask follow-up questions: "Why?" "What made you choose that?"
- [ ] Watch what they do (not just what they say)
- [ ] Thank them sincerely for their time

### DON'Ts âŒ

- [ ] Don't give hints or help too much
- [ ] Don't argue about design choices
- [ ] Don't interrupt their process
- [ ] Don't make them feel bad for struggling
- [ ] Don't say "It's not you, it's the design"
- [ ] Don't use jargon they won't understand
- [ ] Don't ask leading questions
- [ ] Don't multi-task (put phone away!)

## INTRODUCTION SCRIPT

**Read this to each participant:**

---

**Thanks for coming! Here's what we're doing today:**

We've built a website called Silent Auction Gallery for school fundraising. Today, I want to watch you use it so we can make it better.

**Here's the important part**: You're not being tested. THE WEBSITE is being tested. There are no right or wrong answers. If something is confusing, that's helpful feedback - that's exactly what we want to know.

**What will happen:**
1. I'll ask you to complete a few tasks (like browsing auctions or placing bids)
2. Think out loud - tell me what you're thinking as you go
3. If you get stuck, that's okay - just tell me
4. It's totally fine to say "I don't know" or "This is confusing"
5. Takes about an hour total

**Questions before we start?** [Pause]

**Great! Let's begin.**

## DATA COLLECTION

### Quantitative Data
- Task completion rate (yes/no/partial)
- Time per task
- Error count per task
- Rating scores (1-5 scale)
- Success/failure counts

### Qualitative Data
- Direct quotes from participants
- Observations of confusion/delight
- Body language notes
- Questions they ask
- Suggestions they volunteer
- Pain points identified

### Video/Audio Recording
- Record screen + participant face (if consent given)
- Capture verbal feedback
- Timestamps key moments
- Saves re-watching if needed
